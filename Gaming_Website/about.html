About me and the reason for picking this game:

Hello, my name is Gordon Tan and I'm a college student at Seneca taking Computer Programming and Analysis. I've really loved playing video games my entire life since I was a young kid and to to be honest, was a big inspiration for me to get into programming in the first place. I always wondered to myself how do games even operate and work? I started messing around in some basic Flash around Grade 10 and loved it ever since then. Game development on the side has also just been a personal hobby of mine and I really enjoy doing a lot of research into that. I've actually dabbled with game engines like Unity and Unreal Engine. Took some time to delve into openGL to understand how the graphics pipeline works and shaders have always been really cool to me. I love looking at video games from an analytical perspective as much as I enjoy playing them and breaking down how they did things. Final Fantasy 9 is probably out of the entire final fantasy franchise the one I held closest to my heart. Out of at least the 3D final fantasy games, it was always the most interesting one. I'm more of a fan of traditional high fantasy stuff more than anything else in this franchise and the character designs are just so distinct and memorable with it's cartoonier look. My preference has always leaned to more cartoon aesthetics than realistic graphics as that just ages better over time to me. Hence why I was pretty firm on picking this particular game. It's one of those important games that really shaped my taste.
